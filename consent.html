Research Consent Form - Key Points

“The Global A-T Family Data Platform” initiative is a patient-driven effort overseen by a panel of ataxia-telangiectasia (A-T) advocates that will empower A-T patients and their families to help directly transform research and treatment of the disease by sharing their genetic data and related health information with researchers around the world. If families also agree to allow researchers to re-contact them with follow-up research questions, the platform will enable the A-T community to have a continuous dialog with researchers and potentially accelerate progress in A-T research. The ultimate goal of this effort is to create an environment in which the A-T patient community and their families collect their clinical and genetic data and govern sharing this data with researchers to better understand the disease.

 Note: If you are a parent or guardian of an ataxia-telangiectasia patient, the words “you” and “your,” below, refer to your child.

1. What is the purpose of this research initiative?

We want to better understand genetic and clinical factors affecting A-T patients so that we can work toward developing more effective therapies and diagnostics. By partnering directly with patients, and enabling an ongoing dialog with researchers, we will be able to study many more cases worldwide and create a comprehensive and evolving resource for A-T genetics and health characteristics. This is a critical step towards developing potential therapies and improving care.

2. What will I have to do if I agree to participate in this effort?

  • You will answer some questions about your disease and medical care.
  • If you agree, we will contact your doctor to get copies of your medical records, and possibly to confirm your diagnosis.
  • If you agree, we will periodically invite you to participate in additional research studies and post questionnaires about your medical condition and daily life written by researchers that are studying your data. You may choose not to receive these invitations or decline to answer any individual questions.
  • If you decide to participate in the genome sequencing study currently open for enrollment you will send a saliva (spit) sample to us in a pre-stamped package that we will provide.

3. Do I have to participate in this initiative?

No. Taking part in any portion of this initiative is voluntary. Even if you decide to participate, you can always change your mind and remove your data from the platform or ask not to receive additional communication about future studies. Your medical care will not change whether you participate or not.

4. Will I benefit from participating?

Participating in this study may not improve your own health. The information we collect may aid in research efforts to provide better A-T treatment and prevention options to patients in the future. We will provide updates on our website about key research discoveries made possible by your participation. We will not be able to provide you with any specific information about your own genetic data. If, at some point in the future, we believe that analysis of your genetic results may impact or change your diagnosis of A-T, we will contact your doctor, if you agree to this.

5. What are the risks of taking part in this research initiative?

There is a potential risk that your information (which includes information from your health records and possibly your genetic information) could be seen by unauthorized individuals. However, we have procedures and security measures in place designed to minimize this risk and protect the confidentiality of your information.

6. Will it cost me anything to participate in this research initiative?

No.

7. Who will use my samples and see my information?

After removing your name and other readily identifiable information (these items will be only available to the study staff at the A-T Children’s Project), your genomic data and health information will be available to researchers around the world through a secure, access-controlled data repository developed and operated by the Broad Institute of MIT and Harvard. Your data can be only accessed by qualified investigators that have been granted permission by a data access committee selected by the A-T Children’s Project, comprised of A-T family members as well as scientific and medical advisors. We will keep track of every study that has used your information.

8. Can I stop taking part in this research initiative?

Yes, you can withdraw from this research study at any time, and your data will no longer be shared with researchers in future research studies. Your data will be removed from the platform. However, any of your information that has already been shared with investigators cannot be withdrawn from their studies.

9. What if I have questions?

If you have any questions, please send an email to support@atfamilies.org or call USA 954.481.6611 and ask to speak with one of the A-T Children’s Project study staff about this initiative and the data platform.
